Welcome to ELL

We are excited to announce the establishment of the Econometrics Learning Lab (ELL) in the Department of Economics at Indiana University. The ELL aims to develop and disseminate innovative methodologies and econometric theories specifically designed for analyzing large-dimensional economic data.

Our Mission

In today's data-rich environment, there is an urgent need for tools that can effectively handle complex datasets we encounter in economic applications. The ELL is poised to meet this demand by equipping faculty and graduate students with the necessary skills and knowledge to utilize machine learning tools for economic data analysis.

Recent Achievements

From August 19 to 23, the ELL hosted its inaugural workshop, Machine Learning in Economics, attended by six faculty members and thirteen graduate students. The workshop featured comprehensive coverage of advanced topics including:

  • Large dimensional regression techniques (PCA, Ridge, Lasso)
  • Reproducing kernel Hilbert space and kernel methods
  • Support vector machines with linear and kernel tricks
  • Tree-based approaches and neural networks
